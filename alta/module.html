Concept Specific Learner Expectations Notes
Background Information The student should:
understand and be able to explain how cosmic rays were discovered 

 
understand and explain what impact cosmic rays had historically in various areas of physical research 

 
explain the broad scope of the 'radiation spectrum' (both particulate and light) and where various energy cosmic rays fall in this spectrum (example sunlight and particulate solar winds vs. x-ray busts) 

 
explain the basic principles behind the functioning of ALTA and the specific research questions ALTA is designed to test 

 
describe the various other cosmic ray detectors which exist and are being planned/built 

 
summarize the current questions that new detectors are being built to examine and explain a variety of the various competing theories which attempt to answer these questions 
 Sources and references from which students can begin their research will be provided via the Web along with more detailed guides to each of the various research topics and how to proceed.
Applications of Physical Theory to Practical Technology The student should:
demonstrate an understanding of how several experimental devices from the ALTA project apply basic physical theories studied in physics 20 & 30 

 
make appropriate connections between the experimental devices and actual technology used in society 

 
apply their understanding of the basic physical theories seen in these devices to other larger scale applications (eg. particle accelerators and other forms of detectors such as neutrino observatories)
 The list and description of the devices will be supplied via the Web along with material to aid students with the basic understanding and setup of this research project
Scientific Research The student should:
apply their understanding of the physics behind cosmic rays or radiation to create a hypothesis around a proposed problem/question 

 
design an experiment/analysis aimed at verifying or discrediting their hypothesis using a small variety of techniques, instrumentation and data from the ALTA project provided for them 

 
demonstrate an ability to take and record data in a professional scientific manner 

 
perform an analysis of their data using appropriate techniques while demonstrating a basic appreciation for the concepts of experimental error and its various causes

 
demonstrate the ability to properly use the analyzed data in forming a conclusion about both their hypothesis and the general question (eg. does the verification or discrediting of their hypothesis necessarily answer the original question?) 
 Suggested questions for the focus of the students research will be supplied via the Web. Other new research focus questions are encouraged and should be discussed with ALTA project professors for viability and suggested approaches
Scientific Paper The student should:
assemble a scientific paper and presentation making sure both the paper and presentation conform to professional standards for the publication and presentation of scientific papers
 Sample papers of both professional and student origin will appear on the Web.
